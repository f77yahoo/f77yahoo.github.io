SVITER ART GROUP & IVAN SVITLYCHNYI VERBATIX algorithmic environment

curator: Daria Pashchenko

26 January – 5 February 2018

Opening Reception:

26 January 2018, 18.00-22.00

For the first time Ukrainian artists SVITER art-group & Ivan Svitlychnyi are participating in the international festival for art and digital culture transmediale Vorspiel 2018. This group is presenting a site- and sound specific installation in which drum machines and electronic synthesizers create a unique algorithm that converts data inside the gallery into audio tracks in real time. Based on the former project Untitled (2017), presented at the Ukrainian pavilion on the 57th Venice Biennale, VERBATIX has been transforming into an archive, that acquires personal attributes and becomes an equivalent of a hyperlink.

VERBATIX is constantly and currently reshaping itself, it has acquired personal attributes and has begun the transformation to some sort of hyperlink – it combines intermedia with the conditional «text». In this case the «text» becomes an archive: the documentation and information that constantly updated through contextual queries. They provide data array for the generation of algorithmic sound installation. The archive also shapes the visual space, in which using projection and video, all the layers of information appear simultaneously.

Hyperlink represents a new form of interaction with reality. As Lev Manovich wrote in his “Language of new Media”, hyperlinks are the most radical and disruptive innovation of texts. VERBATIX distort information and, being a hyperlink, simultaneously creates a new product - hypertext, an archive of different time frames and spaces. This navigated space is a medium for telling a story which an observer can perceive using the medias of archive.  Hyperlinks transform our interaction with information and increase participant’s perception of news credibility. On the other hand, a distortion take place right at the moment when information is being separated from its source. New media creation technology allows to create new types of illusions, taken at face value. 

SVITER ART GROUP (LERA POLIANSKOVA and MAX ROBOTOV) started in Kharkiv first as a musical project in 2009. Later on, in collaboration with Ivan Svitlychnyi, SVITER founded the artistic initiative 01011101 in 2010, as well as the creative exposition center ТЕЦ in Kharkiv in 2014. In 2015, they founded Shuhliada (The Drawer), the first virtual art space in Ukraine and a platform for the support and integration of independent artistic projects into new media systems. Since 2015, Ivan Svitlychnyi and SVITER have been members of the Institution of Unstable Thoughts, an organization that runs a variety of art projects including maintaining an electronic database of Ukrainian contemporary art.

IVAN SVITLYCHNYI (b.1988) graduated from Kharkiv State Academy of Design and Art majoring in Easel and Monumental Sculpture. He was nominated for the Pinchuk Art Centre Prize repeatedly in 2011, 2013, 2015 and now in 2018. Furthermore, he was the artist-in-residence at Cittadellarte-Fondazione Pistoletto in Biella, Italy in 2014.

DARIA PASHCHENKO is an independent curator from Ukraine, who lives and works in Berlin. Her background experience includes project at leading art institutions of Ukraine such as Art Artsenal, Pinchuk Art Centre, Bottega Gallery, Charitable Foundation  «Development of Ukraine» as well as independent curatorial practice. Founder of the Verum Corpus, an innovative platform where art and technology meet. Currently conducting her own research on the effects of technology on modern body conceptualization.
